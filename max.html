About Us

History and/or background. Caritas is the international Catholic organization whose mission is to be committed to humanitarian aid, social justice, and serving the poor and vulnerable. It was born in 1897 when the first Caritas group in Germany was established by Lorenz Werthmann. In time, Caritas began to expand through Europe, where independent national organizations worked in support of the poor and victims of war conflict. Those national branches later combined to form the officially recognized Caritas Internationalis in 1951. Backed by the Vatican, it has since grown into more than 160 member organisations today, advocating for issues such as hunger, healthcare, disaster relief, and climate change. Caritas works based on Catholic Social Teaching: human dignity, the common good, and solidarity. Caritas continues its mission today: serving the poor and advocating on behalf of a just and compassionate world.

Our Services

Challenges caritas faces and the opportunities it creates for their community. This means that quite a number of challenges beset Caritas in its quest to support vulnerable communities. For one, it has very limited resources to attend to such vast needs among the populations it serves, especially those regions beset by extreme poverty, disaster, or conflict. Another major challenge it faces in some areas is political instability, where government restrictions or unrest impede Caritas from providing aid or engaging in long-term programs. The growing recurrence of disasters in times of weather change challenges the organization on its quest to always offer emergency relief and to find sustainable solutions. Global crises due to war, migration, and health emergencies stretch this capacity further, as Caritas is required to engage in a number of urgent responses simultaneously. In spite of this challenge, Caritas creates enormous opportunities for the communities it serves. Education and training in skills help people enjoy better livelihoods and be self-sufficient through Caritas. Community development, in the instance of provision of improved health care, clean water, and good housing, is used by Caritas to foster long-term growth and stability of a community. This it does through raising awareness on significant issues such as human rights and climate change, therefore pressing towards effective policy changes locally and globally. Moreover, when disasters occur, Caritas is one of the first organizations to offer food, temporary shelter, and medical aid, thus bringing hope to many individuals. All in all, even though the organization faces multiple challenges, it is still bringing change in various communities.

Opportunities for how students can get involved as part of their Faith in Service program. Students may also be involved in various meaningful opportunities to serve Caritas through the Faith in Service program. Events could include serving locally: students may participate by serving at organizations locally that take part in or are affiliated with Caritas, organize food and/or clothing drives, or other community outreach events. This direct involvement allows them to see the impact of their service within their communities and to serve those in need. Other ways of involvement include fundraising activities. The students can initiate or take part in fundraising activities like charity walks, sales of baked goods, or school-wide donation campaigns to help in raising money for the projects Caritas undertakes around the globe. Such fundraising might go toward much-needed projects in disaster relief, hunger alleviation, or educational support for some of the world's most impoverished communities. Students can also participate by organizing or taking part in awareness campaigns: raise awareness of the many global issues on poverty, changes in climate, and social justices affecting people in their school or community as one real way to support the mission of Caritas. This can be done by holding talks, running workshops, creating social media content to relay this message to inspire others to action. Other options involve projects of advocacy and social justice, whereby students, through letters to the government, petitions, or events, might be more actively able to advocate for vulnerable communities and advance the cause of social justice as is expected of any true Christian. The other schools have mission trips or service-learning experiences in collaboration with Caritas, which allow students to visit the communities in need and participate in hands-on projects of school construction, relief work, or supporting sustainable development initiatives. In these experiences, the student not only learns about global challenges but also contributes positively to the change in those environments.
